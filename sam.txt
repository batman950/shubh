title="Ng\xc3\xb4 \xc4\x90\xc3\xacnh C\xe1\xba\xa9n">Ng\xc3\xb4 \xc4\x90\xc3\xacnh C\xe1\xba\xa9n</a></b> (1911\xe2\x80\x931964) was a younger brother and confidant of <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="17th parallel north">17th parallel</a>. Based in the former imperial capital of <a href="/wiki/Hu%E1%BA%BF" title="Hu\xe1\xba\xbf">Hu\xe1\xba\xbf</a>, C\xe1\xba\xa9n earned a reputation as the most oppressive of the Ng\xc3\xb4 brothers, operating private armies and <a href="/wiki/Secret_police" title="Secret police">secret police</a> that controlled the central region. C\xe1\xba\xa9n\'s influence began to wane after his elder brother <a href="/wiki/Ng%C3%B4_%C4%90%C3%ACnh_Th%E1%BB%A5c" title="Ng\xc3\xb4 \xc4\x90\xc3\xacnh Th\xe1\xbb\xa5c">Ng\xc3\xb4 \xc4\x90\xc3\xacnh Th\xe1\xbb\xa5c</a> was appointed the Roman Catholic Archbishop of Hu\xe1\xba\xbf. Th\xe1\xbb\xa5c overshadowed C\xe1\xba\xa9n and aggressively promoted <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholicism">Catholicism</a>, which led to the banning of the <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_flag" title="Buddhist flag">Buddhist flag</a> in 1963 during <a href="/wiki/Vesak" title="Vesak">Vesak</a>, the celebration of the birthday of <a href="/wiki/Gautama_Buddha" title="Gautama Buddha">Gautama Buddha</a>. C\xe1\xba\xa9n\'s forces <a href="/wiki/Hu%E1%BA%BF_Ph%E1%BA%ADt_%C4%90%E1%BA%A3n_shootings" title="Hu\xe1\xba\xbf Ph\xe1\xba\xadt \xc4\x90\xe1\xba\xa3n shootings">opened fire</a> on a crowd protesting the ban, killing nine and precipitating the <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_crisis" title="Buddhist crisis">Buddhist crisis</a> and the eventual toppling of the Diem regime in a <a href="/wiki/1963_South_Vietnamese_coup" title="1963 South Vietnamese coup">November 1963 coup</a>. C\xe1\xba\xa9n was arrested and turned over to the <a href="/wiki/Military_junta" class="mw-redirect" title="Military junta">military junta</a>, which executed him in 1964.
